INTERFACE DESIGN FOR AI LABROBOT

Sean Chartz, Yiran Hu, Anna Jarebro, Shengchen Zhang
Innovation and Design, Tongji University, Shanghai

The goal of this project was to visualise a knowledge graph from a lab assistent AI robot and turn it into a user interface suited for the Chinese market.

Knowledge structure

The first step was to map out what kind of information the robot can store, how it is stored and how we can use it to our advantage in our design.

Scenario

In order to understand the situations where the robot would act and interacts with users we created scenarios based on interviews that assisted the design process.

Level of details

To maintain cohesiveness thought the design we categorised seven different types of interfaces depending on the detail level. Using the users focus to drive semantic zooming when navigating between the different levels.

Personal note: One of the most valuable insights from my exchange at Tongji University, Shanghai is the importance of context and who you are designing for. The way we as consumers are used to process digital information, and what we think of as value, differs significantly across different cultures. Working with designs aimed for the Chinese market with brilliant students from a different educational background really made me grow as a designer.
