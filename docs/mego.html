My role

To start this project we examined competitors in the field, some of the main findings that we brought with us from the analysis were:

Competative analysis

I was part of the creative work of ideating and creating wireframes. I was also responsible for a lot of administrative work such as, setting up meetings and managing hand-ins. Furthermore, I often took a moderating role in the discussions about design decisions.

A problem with zoom is that it’s hard to unmute yourself if you use other apps on your phone simultaneously, which is often the case when you're on the phone.

WeChat had a place where you could collect and access different tools directly from your group chat. We found this really neat as it can ease collaborative work when you are on the phone.

In messenger, you need to minimize the call to access the chat , which means that you might miss parts of a presentation if you want to write in the chat.

It's unnecessary to compete with features of collaborative apps such as Miro and Menti that already work well. Instead, we focused on how we can make them work together.

Challenge

Next up, we ideated and built the app with wireframes. Since this is a completely new product, a challenge at this stage was to create a cohesive direction for the design. One example of this kind of situation was that some of us are more used to design for android and others for IOS. A minor problem indeed, but we still noticed how the different references affected the work.

Solution

We solved this by having small regular checkups with everyone in the team and initiated a small design system. In this way, we could understand everyone's points of view but still maintain efficiency and coherence.

Main takeaways

  • I improved my skills in Figma
  • I learned how to better communicate design decisions and ideas
  • I learned how to better evolve and engage everyone in the team
  • I learned how to divide work among team members and still maintain a cohesive style and design
