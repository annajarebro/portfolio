The online meeting environment due to COVID-19 has increased people's screentime by... well A LOT. A mobile app is a great platform for taking a check-up meeting on the go or when trying to listen in on a presentation and do other things in the meantime. In this project, we designed MeGo. We worked toward the goal: Create an application that allows you to participate in meetings on the go and enable easy switching between other collaborative apps.

My role in this project was both to be a part of the creative work of ideating and creating wireframes, and to be responsible for a lot of administrative work such as, setting up meetings and managing hand-ins. Furthermore, I often took a moderating role in the discussions about design decisions.

Competitive Analysis

To start this project we examined competitors in the field, some of the main findings that we brought with us from the analysis were:

A problem with zoom is that it’s hard to unmute yourself if you use other apps on your phone simultaneously, which is often the case when you're on the phone.

WeChat had a place where you could collect and access different tools directly from your group chat. We found this really neat as it can ease collaborative work when you are on the phone.

In messenger, you need to minimize the call to access the chat , which means that you might miss parts of a presentation if you want to write in the chat.

It's unnecessary to compete with features of collaborative apps such as Miro and Menti that already work well. Instead, we focused on how we can make them work together.

Wireframes

Next up, we ideated and built the app with wireframes. Since this is a completely new product, a challenge at this stage was to create a cohesive direction for the design. One example of this kind of situation was that some of us are more used to design for android and others for IOS. A minor problem indeed, but we still noticed how the different references affected the work. We tried to solve this by having small check ups with everyone, and then dividing into new small teams for each session. In this way, we could understand everyone's points of view but still maintain efficiency.
