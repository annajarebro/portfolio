Problem

No one will read your sustainability sub-page.

In this 3 day UX competition, we created the Co-design club. What we envision is a system where the app and the car are tools that aid the user and Polestar to together shape the future.

How can we meet consumers' needs?

Working together with Hyperlab, we wanted to understand how to create good user experiences when presenting sustainability information. Therefore, I ran a study involving 80 consumers and 2 communication experts to understand consumer needs and current pain points..

Finding the gap

The insights pointed towards four different behavioral archetypes. Anna feels that nudges are good enough to guide her buying intention, Mia, who will spend a lot of time reading up on all available information. But how do we design for the two in the middle? That's what I wanted to explore in my design concept for one of Hyperlab's clients, Safira. (Note: All data in the prototype is fictional)

Early first touch

How do we browse online products? We look at product images. Since consumers won't spend time looking for information, I wanted to include it early and utilize their existing customer journey. Therefore, introducing traceability information in the product images.

Stay on product page

When visiting an online store, the goal is to buy a product. And demand for sustainable goods is increasing, but we shouldn't be navigating further from our goal just to get the information we need. So, a solution that provides more detailed information in a browsable way while remaining on the product page - stories.

Finding the limit

I tested the concept with a couple of users to see what works and understand how complex visualizations can be in this format. The browsing behavior in stories is very rapid. So the information needs to be understood within a few glances.

Exploring stories

I explored different visual and graphical approaches to the stories. But of course, there are endless opportunities to create richer experiences through video and storytelling that suits the brand.
