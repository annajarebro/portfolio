Stravito - summary

Stravito is an knowledge management platform used by market research teams at large enterprises like Burberry, Comcast, and Electrolux. During my time at Stravito I worked with the team responsible for experiences regarding search, upload and machine learning.

Team

  • 1 Designer

  • 1 Product Manager
  • 6 Developers

  • 3 Stakeholder teams

Tl;dr

  • Designed a new feature for uploading and annotating large file systems
  • Increased onboarding capacity and internal productivity
  • Reduced tech involvement by 100%
  • Although we did not achieve 100% self-service, the process became notably faster due to improved efficiency for our Operations team

The problem

Too much tech involvement and inefficient annotation workflow

Each onboarding involved way too many calls with IT departments and fetching data from odd corners of the customer's existing file system. Our operations team then manually annotated their files using old (rather scrappy) internal tools. Scalable? Nah.

Challenge

Designing for efficiency while maintaining high accuracy

Two types of users with conclicting goals. The feature will be used by both operations teams (internal users) and the custumer (external users). Our operations team ensure that the platform was populated with high quality data. They were also responsible for selecting the training data set for our algorithms. The custumer on the other hand wants to get their documents on the platform with as little effort as possible.

Goals

What success looks like

Be able to onboard more custumers

Work less on file transfer and more on product development

Get custumers to launch their platform quickly

Iterations

From auto to guidance

Auto-tagging had still too much uncertainty to be added by default. Therefore, I exprimented with a more manual auto-fill option and uncertainty indications. However, after collaborating with developers we relieased that tracking auto-tagged fields as users starts to interact with docs wouldn't be feasible at this stage. Therefore, we went with a softer qulity control on prioritized areas.

Iterations

File path - Most suprising find

Hypothesis: file path is just for serach, no one actually reads them.

We quilckly realised that we needed to include the file path to make documents more serachable and bulk edit friendly. Initally only showing the last sub-file as we though file paths were mostly used to search, not to read.

However, the person initating buying this platfrom are often the librarian of their team, meaning they are one of the few people who actually know most of their file system by heart. When testing we realised that they relied heavily on file path to annotate.

Results

Increased onboarding capacity and internal productivity

While there is still improvements to be made, introducing this featured reduced tech involvement by 100%. With the first itreation, onboarding is still depentent on Ops and not fully self-served. But the process became much faster as the Ops team became more efficient at annotating documents and performing quality control.
