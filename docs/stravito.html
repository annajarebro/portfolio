Designing a new uploading and annotation feature reducing developer involvement from hundreds to 0 hours when onboarding new customers to Stravito's platform.

Context

Knowledge management platform (SaaS)

Stravito is an knowledge management platform used by market research teams at large enterprises like Burberry, Comcast, and Electrolux. During my time at Stravito I worked with the team responsible for experiences regarding search, upload and machine learning.

Problem

The process for onboarding file systems was not scalable

Onboarding new customers to the platform means migrating anywhere between 300-100k documents. As the company grew, we encountered a scaling bottleneck caused by the manual processes involved in migrating and annotating documents. The process required too much tech involvement, and inefficent annotation workflow for the operations team.

Users

Internal and external users

The feature will be used by both operations teams (internal users) and the custumer (external users). Our operations team ensure that the platform was populated with high quality data. They were also responsible for selecting the training data set for our algorithms. The custumer on the other hand wants to get their documents on the platform with as little effort as possible.

Goals

Business goals

Increase scaling capacity with self-serve document onboarding

Increase developer productivity by minimizing tech involvement

External user goals

Onboard documents to platform with minimum effort

Role out platform to organisation ASAP

Longterm goal: high adoption and usage within organisation

Internal user goals

Achive good auto-tagging by deliver high quality training data

How might we make it effortless and quick to onboard documetns while maintaining high accuracy?

Early ideation

Guidance, auto-fill and bulk edit

Started by gathering existing knowledge from Product Manager, CS and Ops team. The product manager and I also had an early ideation to align our vision.

Iterations

From auto to guidance

Auto-tagging had still too much uncertainty to be added by default. Therefore, I exprimented with a more manual auto-fill option and uncertainty indications. However, after collaborating with developers we relieased that tracking auto-tagged fields as users starts to interact with docs wouldn't be feasible at this stage. Therefore, we went with a softer qulity control on prioritized areas.

Iterations

File path - Most suprising find

Hypothesis: file path is just for serach, no one actually reads them.

We quilckly realised that we needed to include the file path to make documents more serachable and bulk edit friendly. Initally only showing the last sub-file as we though file paths were mostly used to serach, not to read.

However, the person initating buying this platfrom are often the librarian of their team, meaning they are one of the few people who actually know most of their file system by heart. When testing we realised that they relied heavily on file path to annotate.

Results

Increased onboarding capacity and internal productivity

While there is still improvements to be made, introducing this featured reduced tech involvement by 100%. With the first itreation, onboarding is still depentent on Ops and not fully self-served. But the process became much faster as the Ops team became more efficient at annotating documents and performing quality control.
