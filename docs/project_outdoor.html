WEBSITE CONCEPT

A website concept designed for a small outdoor company.

Anna Jarebro
Webdesign 7,5hp
University of Uppsala

My design approach

The scope of this project was to choose a website, analyze the current UI, and make a redesign. After analyzing the existing site, I formulated the design goal: Create a clear overview of what the company offers in a way that’s inspiring and easy to navigate. One of the main difficulties to overcome was designing with a goal-directed approach without having any direct contact with the company. To solve this problem, I dug around on social media and travel sites to look at reviews and comments. Through this, I started to map out who their customers are and what their goal of visiting the website could be.

The users

I prefer to use behaivoral personas rather than demographic ones. Personally, I think personas based one behaivour says a lot more about the goals the user might have. For this project I worked toward three main behaivours:

The Tourists: Wants to book an activity for the entire family that's available on the specific dates they're visiting.

The Bear Grylls: Wants to dig deep into a specific course and for example book bushcraft basics.

The team builders: Wishes to make a request for a tailord activity for a larger group of people.

Wireframes

To plan the structure and content of the website I began with sketching low fidelity wireframes. Then moving on to build digital wireframs that evolved until it reached a level of high fidelity.

The fonts

When choosing fonts, the goal was to enhance the outdoor vibe and still make it clean and readable. Therefore, choosing two contrasting fonts.

The colors

By using dark shades of green and orange the colours get a more worn look, like that outdoor jacket that you inherited from your grandpa that’s still going strong.
