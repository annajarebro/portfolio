The problem with sub-pages

In e-commerce, sustainability information is usually found on a sub-page accompanied by a few generic labels on the product page. However, customers often overlook sustainability sub-pages, and labels stating e.g. "sustainable choice" is becoming ineffective for many consumers, as it has been linked to greenwashing.

Our goal

Increase engagement with traceability information, making retailers with high transparency in their supply chain more competitive. To achieve this, we aimed to understand how to make customers engage with traceability information and how to present it in a way that makes sense to people and assists them in making purchasing decisions.

My contribution

I wrote my thesis solo, so it was a fantastic project to build my designer stamina. I managed all aspects of planning and executiona and making decisions on when to move forward and when to go back and interate. I conducted a study involving 78 consumers and 2 communication experts to understand consumer needs and current pain points, utilizing methods such as surveys, interviews, and focus groups. I explored different design patterns and created and tested design concepts.

Mapping out the current process and user needs

Before creating any wireframes, I mapped the current process of onboarding customers into the platform, including touchpoints with teams and customers, as well as information and document flow.

How I enhanced cross-function collaboration

As I started to sync with developers, we realized that most of the explored features would not be possible to include in the beta version. We faced quite a few technical constraints regarding document uploading, synchronization to our platform, etc. So, most of what was inherited from early exploration was the idea of going for a table view to enable fast annotation.

The straw man approach

I made quick and rough versions of different interaction patterns to generate discussion and create alignment.

Sync quiz

I tried a "multiple-choice" approach to discuss technical constraints as a method to help me ask the right questions and document the answers quickly.

Lo-fi video recordings

When asynchronous communication was needed video prototypes were often the easiest way to discuss interaction patterns with developers.

How might we incorporate help and guidance?

One of the most interesting design challenges during this project was how we could add help and guidance similar to what the implementation team provides in-person to ensure a positive onboarding experience.

Most surprising insight from beta testing

As we launched and tested the beta version, we realized that the file path was more significant for uploads than we initially thought. At first, we belived that only the parent folder name mattered. However, we came to realize that the person initiating the purchase of an insights platform often played the role of a "librarian" within their team, knowing the folder tree inside out. As a result, they used the file path as a quick reference for annotation.
