INTERFACE DESIGN FOR AI LABROBOT

Sean Chartz, Yiran Hu, Anna Jarebro, Shengchen Zhang
Innovation and Design, Tongji University, Shanghai

The goal of this project was to visualize the knowledge graph of a lab assistant AI robot and turn it into a user interface suited for the Chinese market.

Knowledge structure

The first step was to map out what kind of information the robot can store, how it is stored, and how we can use it to our advantage in our design. To better understand what information that could be withdrawn from the robot, we created a database to represent a mock-up of the KG.

Scenario

To understand the situations where the robot would act and interacts with users, we created scenarios based on interviews that assisted the design process.

Level of details

To maintain cohesiveness through the design, we categorized seven different types of interfaces depending on the level of detail. Using user focus to drive semantic zooming when navigating between the different levels.

Personal note: One of the most valuable insights from my exchange at Tongji University, Shanghai is the importance of context and knowing who you are designing for. The way we as consumers are used to process digital information, and what we think of as value, differs significantly across different cultures. Working in a completely different culture with highly diverse teams really made me grow as a designer.
